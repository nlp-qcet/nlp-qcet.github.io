QOC-f : Correctness of outputs in their own right, Form

Node Details
QOC-f-1 : Grammaticality

Definition:
A better system produces texts with fewer grammatical errors
Elicitation:
Intrinsic, subjective, relative: Which of these texts has fewer grammatical errors, looking at their form only and ignoring their content/meaning?
Intrinsic, subjective, absolute: To what degree is this text free of grammatical errors, looking at its form only and ignoring its content/meaning?
Attestations:
Humphreys et al., 2001 informally evaluate 200 outputs manually for Grammaticality reporting 4% of outputs with grammatical errors for their combined parser/generator.
Additional notes and information:
[QOC-f-1] is Grammaticality as judged by native speakers, i.e. it's a human-assessable only QC. Cf. [QIC-f-1] Matching Syntactic Structure (given in input), and [QEC-f-2] Adherence to Syntactic Rules which can be assessed either with metrics or humans.
