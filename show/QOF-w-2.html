QOF-w-2 : Poeticness/Non-poeticness (outputs as a whole)

Definition:
A better system produces outputs that are either (a) more poetic, or (b) less poetic
Elicitation:
Intrinsic, subjective, relative: Which of these outputs is more poetic?
Intrinsic, subjective, absolute: To what degree is this output poetic?
Attestations:
Xie et al., 2017 ask evaluators to assess the overall poeticness of generated poems, finding e.g. that poems generated by more complex LSTMs are perceived as more poetic than those generated by simple LSTMs.
Additional notes and information:
(Non)poeticness of outputs captures the degree to which outputs have the characteristics of a poem, including alliteration, rhythm, rhyming and specific syllable/metre patterns, metaphor, topic and narrative structure. For [QOF-w-2], this is assessed without taking the input or anything external to the system into account.
