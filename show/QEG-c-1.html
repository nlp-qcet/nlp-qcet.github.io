QEG-c-1 : Naturalness (content/meaning)

Definition:
A better system produces texts that are in their content/meaning more natural in a given context and/or for a given subset of speakers
Attestations:
Xu et al., 2021 extract event chains from narrative texts and connect them as a graph. To evaluate the quality of the graph, they randomly sample 500 edges and calculate the proportion of edges which are ``suitable for chatting," finding that to be the case for 73.6% of edges.
Additional notes and information:
If an output is not natural in content, then it isn't likely to be encountered with this content in the given scenario. Cf. [QEG-f-2] Appropriateness: if a text is not appropriate in content then it shouldn't have this content in the scenario.
