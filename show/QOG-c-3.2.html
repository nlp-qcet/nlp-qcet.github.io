QOG-c-3.2 : Cohesiveness

Definition:
A better system produces outputs whose content/meaning elements are linked better
Attestations:
Eisenstadt & Elhadad, 2020 compare WebNLG outputs generated by (i) T5, and (ii) T5 plus a neural micro-planner, with the human target output texts in terms of the counts of cohesive devices they contain, finding that texts generated with a micro-planner are much more similar in this respect to human-written texts.
Additional notes and information:
[QOG-c-3.2] Cohesiveness is all about linkage between content elements at every level of granularity, typically a property of text, it involves discourse connectives, anaphoric referencing, lexical congruity, etc. It is a necessary, but not sufficient, condition for [QOG-c-3] Coherence.
