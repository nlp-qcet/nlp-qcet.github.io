QOF-c : *Feature of outputs in their own right, content/meaning

Node Details
QOF-c-1 : Diversity/Non-diversity (content/meaning)

Definition:
A better system produces outputs that are in their content/meaning either (a) more diverse, or (b) less diverse
Elicitation:
Intrinsic, subjective, relative: Which of these outputs is more diverse in its content/meaning, looking at its content/meaning only and ignoring its form?
Intrinsic, subjective, absolute: To what degree is this output diverse, looking at its content/meaning only and ignoring its form?
Attestations:
Chen et al., 2024 use mean cosine similarity between SBERT embeddings to assess the semantic diversity of generated poems, finding e.g. that their German poems are on the whole substantially more diverse than the English ones.
Additional notes and information:
(Non)diversity of content/meaning captures the variedness of the content/meaning of outputs either at the level of individual outputs where those are longer than a sentence, or at the level of a sample of outputs. Varied content/meaning could be diverse topics, information, or events, etc.
