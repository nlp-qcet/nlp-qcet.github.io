QOC-c-1 : Semantic Correctness

Definition:
A better system produces outputs with fewer semantic errors
Elicitation:
Intrinsic, subjective, relative: Which of these outputs has fewer semantic errors?
Intrinsic, subjective, absolute: To what degree is this text free of semantic errors?
Attestations:
Lindberg et al., 2013 ask an education specialist to assess questions generated by their system in terms of semantic validity, finding e.g. that 66% of them ``made sense.''
Additional notes and information:
Semantic correctness is about the output being logically sound, but also obeying common-sense knowledge about the real world and events occurring in the right temporal order.
