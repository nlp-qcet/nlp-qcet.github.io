QOF-w-1 : Diversity/Non-diversity (outputs as a whole)

Definition:
A better system produces outputs that are either (a) more diverse, or (b) less diverse
Elicitation:
Intrinsic, subjective, relative: Which of these outputs is more diverse?
Intrinsic, subjective, absolute: To what degree is this output diverse?
Attestations:
Jin & Le, 2016 ask evaluators to assess the overall diversity of a set of questions generated by a system from a given input text.

Li et al., 2015 assess the diversity of the responses produced by their conversational system with the distinct-1 and distinct-2 metrics computed as the number of distinct unigrams (bigrams) over the total number of generated tokens.
Additional notes and information:
(Non)diversity of outputs as a whole captures diversity of form and content both, either at the level of individual outputs where those are longer than a sentence, or at the level of a sample of outputs. E.g. a diverse set of questions and answers generated for a given text passage would have little overlap in coverage of the text between them.
