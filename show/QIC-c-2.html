QIC-c-2 : Absence of Additions (relative to input)

Definition:
A better system produces outputs that contain fewer content/meaning units not provided in the input
Elicitation:
Intrinsic, subjective, relative: Which of these texts contains more the content units not provided in the input?
Intrinsic, subjective, absolute: To what degree does this text contain content units not provided in the input?
Attestations:
Cripwell et al., 2023 evaluate the semantic accuracy of the outputs of data-to-text systems in under-resourced languages using several criteria, one of them being the absence of additions. Human evaluators are shown the structured data input and an output text and are asked ``Looking at the Text, is all of its content expressed in the Data expression? (Allow duplication of content.)''
Additional notes and information:
Absence of Additions is defined for cases where Absence of Omissions, as its inverse, is also defined. This will typically hold in those cases where the output is supposed to contain/cover/verbalise all and only content/items/formal representations provided in the input. Cf. the three Nonreduncy QGs, [QOG-f-1], [QOG-c-1], and [QOG-w-1], where omission is not defined.
