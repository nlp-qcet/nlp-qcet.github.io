QOF-f-3 : Complexity/Non-complexity (form)

Definition:
A better system produces outputs that are in their form either (a) more complex, or (b) less complex
Elicitation:
Intrinsic, subjective, relative: Which of these outputs is more complex in its form, looking at its form only and ignoring its content/meaning?
Intrinsic, subjective, absolute: To what degree is this output complex in its form, looking at its form only and ignoring its content/meaning?
Attestations:
In their evaluation of a text simplification system for Spanish, Saggion et al., 2015 measure the syntactic complexity of a sentence as the maximum distance between root and leaf nodes in the dependency tree of the sentence.
Additional notes and information:
Complexity of form captures aspects of output complexity irrespective of meaning, e.g. longer word and sentence length, nested syntactic structure, and low frequency words can all be indicative of higher complexity of form.
