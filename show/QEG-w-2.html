QEG-w-2 : Appropriateness (outputs as a whole)

Definition:
A better system produces texts that are more appropriate in a given context and/or for a given subset of speakers or audience
Attestations:
Macdonald & Siddharthan, 2016 compare the outputs of two news summarisers, one producing basic summaries and the other producing summaries suitable for children. They ask human participants the following question: ``Overall, which of these summaries do you believe is more suitable for a child?''
Additional notes and information:
If an output is not appropriate, then it shouldn't be used in the given scenario. Cf. [QEG-f-1] Naturalness: if an output is not natural then it isn't likely to be encountered in the scenario, but it's not the case that it shouldn't be.
