QOG-c : Goodness of outputs in their own right, content/meaning

Node Details
QOG-c-3 : Coherence

Definition:
A better system produces outputs whose contents/meaning hang(s) together better
Elicitation:
Intrinsic, subjective, relative: Which of these outputs' content/meaning hang(s) together better, looking at their content/meaning only and ignoring their form?
Intrinsic, subjective, absolute: To what degree does this output's content/meaning hang(s) together better, looking at its content/meaning only and ignoring its form?
Attestations:
In the 2005 DUC shared task on summarisation (Dang, 2005), outputs are assessed in terms of the degree to which they meet the following statement: ``The summary should be well-structured and well-organized. The summary should not just be a heap of related information, but should build from sentence to sentence to a coherent body of information about a topic.''
