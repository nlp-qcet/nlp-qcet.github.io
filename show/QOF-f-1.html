QOF-f-1 : Diversity/Non-diversity (form)

Definition:
A better system produces outputs that are in their form either (a) more diverse, or (b) less diverse
Elicitation:
Intrinsic, subjective, relative: Which of these outputs is more more diverse in its form, looking at its form only and ignoring its content/meaning?
Intrinsic, subjective, absolute: To what degree is this output diverse in its form, looking at its form only and ignoring its content/meaning?
Attestations:
Chen et al., 2024 compare the average type-token ratio (ATTR) of poems generated with a range of models, finding e.g. that humans, poetry-specific models and general models have broadly similar ATTR scores.
Additional notes and information:
Diversity of form captures the variedness of the form of outputs either at the level of individual outputs where those are longer than a sentence, or at the level of a sample of outputs. Varied form could be diverse sentence structures, format, or speech patterns, etc.
