QOF-f-4 : Formality/Informality

Definition:
A better system produces outputs that are either (a) more formal, or (b) less formal
Elicitation:
Intrinsic, subjective, relative: Which of these outputs is more formal, looking at its form only and ignoring its content/meaning?
Intrinsic, subjective, absolute: To what degree is this output formal, looking at its form only and ignoring its content/meaning?
Attestations:
Abu Sheikha & Inkpen, 2011 asked human annotators to label system outputs from their template-based, formality-controlled text generator as either formal or informal.
Additional notes and information:
(In)formality captures how relaxed the language of outputs is: in a conversation with friends, or on social media, language tends to be more informal, whereas in academic articles or legal contexts, it tends to be much more formal.
