QOG-c-3.1 : Wellorderedness

Definition:
A better system produces outputs whose content/meaning is ordered better
Attestations:
In their content-planning enhanced approach to dialogue, Xu et al., 2021 evaluate content ordering by first manually segmenting a dialogue by topic, and then rating each segment 1 if the ordering is appropriate, otherwise 0, and reporting the average.
Additional notes and information:
[QOG-c-3.1] Wellorderedness captures whether content is ordered in a way that makes sense, e.g. that events are presented in the right order, that related points are made in the right place(s), etc. It is a necessary, but not sufficient, condition for [QOG-c-3] Coherence.
