QOC-w-1 : Correctness of Outputs (outputs as a whole)

Definition:
A better system produces outputs with fewer overall errors
Elicitation:
Intrinsic, subjective, relative: Which of these outputs has fewer overall errors?
Intrinsic, subjective, absolute: To what degree is this output free of overall errors?
Attestations:
Rafatbakhsh et al., 2021 manually determine the proportion of acceptable multiple-choice language learning items generated by their system; the assessment involved checking that question and answers matched, and that there were the right number of answers.
Additional notes and information:
Correctness of Outputs is often about whether the output is of the correct type, e.g. in a question generator, is the output a question, or if an LLM is prompted for class labels, is the output a class label. The notion of correctness often derives simply from the system task (as in both these examples).
