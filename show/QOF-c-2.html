Node Details
QOF-c-2 : Poeticness/Non-poeticness (content/meaning)

Definition:
A better system produces outputs that are in their content/meaning either (a) more poetic, or (b) less poetic
Elicitation:
Intrinsic, subjective, relative: Which of these outputs is more diverse in its content/meaning, looking at its content/meaning only and ignoring its form?
Intrinsic, subjective, absolute: To what degree is this output diverse in its content/meaning, looking at its content/meaning only and ignoring its form?
Attestations:
Wu et al., 2019 ask evaluators to assess ``whether some part of the poem is imaginative and/or moving'' in a 4-step assessment procedure which is then mapped to a single score.
Additional notes and information:
(Non)poeticness of form captures the degree to which outputs have the semantic characteristics of a poem, including metaphor, topic and narrative structure. For [QOF-c-2], this is assessed without taking the input or anything external to the system into account.
