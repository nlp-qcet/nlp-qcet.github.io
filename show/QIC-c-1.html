QIC-c-1 : Absence of Omissions (relative to input)

Definition:
A better system produces outputs that lack fewer of the content/meaning units provided in the input
Elicitation:
Intrinsic, subjective, relative: Which of these texts lacks fewer of the content units provided in the input?
Intrinsic, subjective, absolute: To what degree does this text contain the content units provided in the input?
Attestations:
Gonz√°lez Corbelle et al., 2022 assess their transformer-based weather forecast generator e.g. with a metric that computes the number of output texts with omissions (input elements not literally contained in the output), finding omissions in 160 out of 272 texts (58%).
Additional notes and information:
Absence of Omissions is defined for cases where Absence of Additions, as its inverse, is also defined. This will typically hold in those cases where the output is supposed to contain/cover/verbalise all and only content/items/formal representations provided in the input. Cf. [QIC-f-2] Inclusion of Keywords where addition is not defined.
