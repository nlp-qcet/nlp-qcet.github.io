Node Details
QOG-w-5 : Understandability

Definition:
A better system produces outputs that are more understandable
Elicitation:
Intrinsic, subjective, relative: Which of these outputs is more understandable?
Intrinsic, subjective, absolute: To what degree is this output understandable?
Attestations:
Hershenhouse et al., 2024 assess LLMs in terms of their ability to communicate medical information to the public by asking crowdworkers to demonstrate their understanding of generated texts through multiple-choice questions.
Additional notes and information:
Understandability captures whether an output can be understood and is commonly evaluated in terms of whether it has been understood (via comphrehension questions). Cf. sub-QC [QOG-w-5.1] Clarity for which an output is assessed in terms of the higher-threshold criterion whether it can be easily understood.
