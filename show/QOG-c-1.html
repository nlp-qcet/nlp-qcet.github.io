QOG-c-1 : Nonredundancy (content/meaning)

Definition:
A better system produces outputs with less redundancy in their content/meaning
Elicitation:
Intrinsic, subjective, relative: Which of these outputs has less redundancy in its content/meaning, looking at their content/meaning only and ignoring their form?
Intrinsic, subjective, absolute: To what degree is this output free of redundancy in its content/meaning, looking at its content/meaning only and ignoring its form?
Attestations:
Di Fabbrizio et al., 2014 evaluate the `compactness' of review summaries on a 5-point scale via human evaluation on Amazon Mechanical Turk, where a compact summary is one that ``does not repeat information.''
Additional notes and information:
Examples of redundancies of content/meaning include the same meaning being expressed more than once in different ways, use of full names when pronouns would suffice, and overly explanatory details (e.g. They closed the door behind them using the doorhandle which was affixed to the door.).
