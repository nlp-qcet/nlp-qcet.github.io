Node Details
QOF-f-2 : Poeticness/Non-poeticness (form)

Definition:
A better system produces outputs that are in their form either (a) more poetic, or (b) less poetic
Elicitation:
Intrinsic, subjective, relative: Which of these outputs is more poetic in its form, looking at its form only and ignoring its content/meaning?
Intrinsic, subjective, absolute: To what degree is this output poetic in its form, looking at its form only and ignoring its content/meaning?
Attestations:
Xie et al., 2017 manually compare four LSTM variants in terms of the quality of rhyme and metre, finding that gated and CNN-based LSTMs perform better than word and character LSTMs.
Additional notes and information:
(Non)poeticness of form captures the degree to which outputs have the formal characteristics of a poem, including alliteration, rhythm, rhyming and specific syllable/metre patterns. For [QOF-f-2], this is assessed without taking the input or anything external to the system into account.
